How to use Meeting Bot

Getting started Sign in to access your bots
  • 1Click Sign In with Google on the landing page. This identifies you so your recordings stay private — only you can see your bots.
  • 2After signing in you land on the Schedule Bot tab. You can start deploying bots immediately — no further setup required.
  • 3To also see your upcoming calendar meetings, click the Google Calendar tab and connect your calendar. This is optional.
  • 4Use Log Out in the top-right corner to end your session. Disconnect on the Google Calendar tab only removes calendar sync — it does not log you out.
Privacy: Each user sees only their own recordings. Recordings are automatically deleted 72 hours after the meeting ends — download or save anything you need before then.
Google Calendar Auto-schedule from your calendar (optional)
  • 1Click Connect Google Calendar to grant read-only access so the bot can find Teams, Zoom, and Google Meet links in your upcoming events.
  • 2Your upcoming meetings that contain a Teams, Zoom, or Google Meet join link appear in the list with the meeting title, time, and platform badge.
  • 3Click Schedule Bot next to any meeting. The bot will automatically join at the scheduled start time and begin recording.
  • 4Once scheduled, the button changes to ✓ Scheduled with a Cancel option. Click Cancel at any time before the meeting starts.
  • 5Use Refresh to re-sync your calendar if you've added new meetings since the page loaded.
Tip: Only meetings with a Teams, Zoom, or Google Meet join link in the invite will appear — events without a link are skipped automatically.
Schedule Bot Deploy a bot to any meeting URL
  • 1Paste a Teams, Zoom, or Google Meet URL into the Meeting URL field. teams.microsoft.com, zoom.us/j/, and meet.google.com links are all supported.
  • 2Bot Name is optional — defaults to "[Your Name]'s Bot". Participants in the meeting lobby will see this name when the bot requests to join.
  • 3Leave Schedule For blank to have the bot join immediately, or set a date and time to have it join automatically at the start of the meeting.
  • 4Click Deploy Bot. A confirmation appears and the bot will join (or wait to join) the meeting.
  • 5After the meeting, go to Meeting History to view the transcript and recording.
Tip: For Teams meetings the bot must be admitted from the lobby by a meeting participant — it won't join automatically if nobody admits it.
Meeting History Review recordings and transcripts
  • 1Shows your bots — past and present — pulled live from Recall.ai. Other users' recordings are not visible.
  • 2Each entry shows the meeting title (or URL), platform badge, bot status, and storage age with a colour-coded cost indicator.
  • 3Status badges show the bot's lifecycle: In Call, Done, Joining, Scheduled, Cancelled, etc.
  • 4Click View on any completed meeting to open the full transcript (speaker-labelled) and play back the recording.
  • 5Click Delete to remove the recording manually at any time. Recordings are automatically deleted after 72 hours — no action needed if you don't need them.
72-hour policy: All recordings and media are automatically purged 72 hours after the meeting ends. Download the recording or transcript before that window closes if you need them.
